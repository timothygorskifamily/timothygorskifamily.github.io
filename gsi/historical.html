Data Sources & Methodological Disclaimers

PROTOTYPE ONLY: This dynamic charting tool is a theoretical backtesting prototype designed exclusively for institutional modeling and discussion. It does not represent an actual fund, offering, or past performance of any real-world asset. All outputs are simulated based on historical indices, proxy assumptions, and mathematical models.

Data Sources & Benchmark Proxies
  • S&P 500 Data (SPY): Equity returns are modeled using the S&P 500 Total Return Index (inclusive of perfectly reinvested dividends), proxied via SPY ETF data or interpolated historical index data prior to ETF inception. It assumes zero tax friction on dividend reinvestment.
  • Risk-Free Rates & Short Rates: The 10-Year Treasury Constant Maturity rate (via St. Louis Fed / FRED) is used to calculate the option budget and forward pricing. The Secured Overnight Financing Rate (SOFR) is used as the base floating crediting rate. For historical periods before SOFR's publication (2018), 3-Month US Treasury Bill yields or Federal Funds rates are used as backward-calculated proxies.
  • Private Credit Spreads: Because granular, standardized private credit data does not exist spanning 50 years, the engine utilizes the ICE BofA US High Yield Index Option-Adjusted Spread (OAS) as a base. It applies a dynamic, user-defined "Illiquidity/Complexity Premium" (e.g., 2.5% to 3.5%) to proxy direct lending returns. It also assumes volatility dampening compared to public high yield, capturing only 40% of extreme public spread blowouts.
  • Private Equity Benchmark (PE): Private Equity returns are modeled using a static historical array intended to proxy average annual US Buyout fund performance (e.g., Cambridge Associates US Private Equity Index equivalents). PE data is inherently subject to appraisal smoothing, survivorship bias, and reporting lags. The model mathematically applies a strict "1.5 and 20" fee structure (1.5% annual management fee deducted monthly, 20% performance carry on profits over principal at Year 10) to simulate net-to-LP returns.
  • Core Bonds Benchmark: Bond benchmark returns are modeled using a static historical array proxying the Bloomberg US Aggregate Bond Index (or similar core aggregate indices). The model assumes a pure buy-and-hold total return with perfectly reinvested coupon payments and assumes 0% management fees or transaction costs for the bond benchmark.
  • Volatility Data (VIX): Options are priced using historical implied volatility, proxied by the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) where available. For periods predating the inception of the VIX (pre-1990), historical realized volatility metrics and simulated flat-vol proxies are used to estimate pricing parameters.
Structural & Mathematical Assumptions
  • Options Pricing Mechanics: Option allocations and Mark-to-Market (MTM) values are calculated continuously using the Black-Scholes European Call model. This model intrinsically assumes log-normal price distributions, constant volatility, a constant risk-free rate within the pricing step, and the inability to exercise the option before the 10-year maturity date.
  • Zero Trading Friction: The model assumes zero transaction costs. It does not account for slippage, bid-ask spreads, broker commissions, or the lack of liquidity that often plagues 10-year OTC derivatives. It assumes you can buy the exact calculated fractional amount of exposure at perfectly efficient mid-market prices.
  • Liquidity and Deployment Constraints: The model operates on the theoretical assumption of 100% Day-1 liquidity. It assumes all capital is perfectly deployed into options and the credit floor exactly at the start of the 120-month vintage. It completely ignores capital call mechanics, ramp-up periods, J-curves, lock-ups, or cash drag typical of real-world private market alternative funds.
  • Fee Accounting and Escrow Compounding: When Annual MTM Carry is enabled, the model employs strict "Source-Based Accounting." Performance fees are immediately subtracted from the interest-bearing credit floor principal to prevent "ghost interest." If the escrow feature is active, siphoned fees are tracked in a separate reserve that assumes perfect reinvestment and compounds continuously at the exact private credit yield, with zero un-invested cash drag.
  • Default Risk & Credit Losses: The modeled private credit yields are assumed to be "Net of Defaults." It assumes either zero defaults, or that the portfolio is so perfectly diversified and structured that the modeled spread fully absorbs and accounts for any underlying loan losses. A real-world concentration of defaults would severely impact the principal protection floor.
  • Tax and Regulatory Constraints: All returns shown are strictly Gross of Investor-Level Taxes. The complex carry structures, high-water marks, options taxation rules (e.g., Section 1256 contracts), and escrow clawbacks do not account for partnership tax accounting, phantom income, or specific regulatory hurdles that would apply to a live structured product.
  • Simulated Macro Scenarios: Vintages explicitly marked with the "[SIM]" tag indicate that historical gaps in specific macro data feeds have been patched using mathematical simulations, interpolated backward curves, or historical averages. These do not represent real-world events.

Past performance, simulated or actual, is no guarantee of future results. Market conditions during future stress events may differ materially from historical backtests, resulting in partial or complete loss of capital.
